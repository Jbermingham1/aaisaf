# TA01.005 — Multi-Turn Prompt Manipulation

## Overview

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **ID** | TA01.005 |
| **Tactic** | [Prompt Manipulation](../../tactics/TA01-prompt-manipulation.md) |
| **Severity** | High |
| **AISS Score** | 7.2 |
| **System Types** | A, B, C, D, E, F, G |

## Description

An attacker gradually shifts the AI system's behaviour over the course of multiple conversation turns, incrementally moving the model from compliant behaviour toward policy-violating outputs. Unlike single-turn injection (TA01.001) which attempts to override instructions immediately, multi-turn manipulation exploits the model's tendency to maintain conversational consistency and build on established context.

The "crescendo attack," formally documented by Microsoft researchers in 2024, is the canonical example: the attacker begins with benign questions, progressively escalates toward sensitive territory, and exploits the model's desire to remain helpful and consistent with the conversation trajectory. By the time the model is producing harmful content, each individual step was a small deviation from the previous turn, making it difficult for per-turn safety classifiers to detect.

Multi-turn manipulation is particularly effective against systems with conversation memory or long context windows, as the accumulated context creates a strong bias toward continuation of the established pattern.

## Attack Vector

**Prerequisites:**
- The attacker has sustained conversational access to the AI system (not single-query API)
- The AI system maintains conversation context across turns
- Safety evaluation is primarily per-turn rather than conversation-level

**Procedure:**
1. Begin with entirely benign, on-topic questions to establish rapport and normalcy
2. Gradually introduce tangential topics that are adjacent to the restricted area
3. Ask the model to acknowledge or expand on increasingly specific details, building a conversational trajectory
4. At each step, reference prior model responses to establish consistency pressure ("As you mentioned earlier...", "Building on what you said about...")
5. Introduce the target sensitive topic as a natural continuation of the established conversation
6. The model produces content it would refuse if asked directly, because the conversational context makes refusal feel inconsistent with its prior responses
7. Optionally, if the model resists, backtrack slightly and approach from a different angle, maintaining the gradual escalation

## Detection

- Implement conversation-level safety evaluation, not just per-turn analysis
- Monitor for gradual topic drift toward restricted categories over multiple turns
- Track the "distance" between conversation start topic and current topic — flag conversations with unusual trajectory
- Detect patterns of incremental escalation: each turn slightly more sensitive than the last
- Analyse user behaviour patterns that match known multi-turn attack playbooks (benign start, gradual pivot, escalation, extraction)

## Remediation

- **Conversation-level safety classifiers**: Evaluate the full conversation trajectory, not just the current turn in isolation
- **Topic drift detection**: Implement automated monitoring for conversations that progressively move toward restricted categories
- **Periodic safety reassessment**: Periodically re-evaluate the conversation context against safety policies, not just at each turn boundary
- **Context window safety anchoring**: Ensure safety instructions remain prominently weighted regardless of conversation length
- **Escalation breakpoints**: Introduce hard safety checkpoints at defined intervals or when topic sensitivity increases
- **Turn-over-turn consistency analysis**: Flag conversations where the model's outputs are progressively deviating from its initial safety posture
